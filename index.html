How We Score

Your match percentage reflects how well a camera fits your shooting style. Not a spec sheet ranking. Not a paid placement. Here's what goes into it.

The Short Version

  • Every answer you give adjusts what matters in the score
  • Match % is about your needs, not an objective "best camera" rating
  • Budget filters what shows up, but doesn't affect match %
  • Two people with different answers will get completely different results
  • No sponsors, no paid placements, no affiliate bias in scoring

How It Works

1

You tell us what you shoot

Weddings, docs, YouTube, commercials ‚Äî each genre has different demands. A wedding shooter needs reliable AF and dual slots. A narrative filmmaker might not care about AF at all. We weight specs based on what actually matters for your work.

High impact
2

We match against real-world specs

Not marketing specs. We use continuous recording battery life (not CIPA ratings), actual AF tracking performance (not just "phase detect"), and real data rates for storage calculations. If a camera says "4K120" but it overheats after 10 minutes, that matters.

Core logic
3

Your preferences shift the weights

Said you need rock-solid autofocus? AF becomes a heavy factor. Prefer manual focus? We stop penalizing cameras without it. Solo shooter? Stabilization and compact size get a boost. Every answer reshapes what the score prioritizes.

Personalization layer
4

Cameras get ranked within your budget

Budget determines which cameras show up, but it doesn't inflate or deflate match percentages. A $1,500 camera and a $5,000 camera are scored the same way against your needs. The percentage is purely about fit.

Budget = filter, not score
